Over the past few decades, the United States has seen a rise in suicide rates despite the increase in awareness and available resources. This dataset tracks the trend from 1999 to 2019 and includes the Age-Adjusted Rate which is used for this visualization.


The left chart argues that the suicide rate is rising. This is done by plotting the average rate across all the states on a yearly basis (shown in red) and each individual state's rate (shown in light grey). This was done to put into perspective the maximum rates in order to amplify the argument.

The right chart argues that the suicide rate is not rising. By changing the domain of the y axis to fit a wider set of values, the plotted line gives the appearance of staying somewhat consistent or at least trending upward an insignificant ammount. This is also done by selecting a few states from the set whos trends were slower than the rest.
